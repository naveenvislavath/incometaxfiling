Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about GST services and compliance.

When is my business required to register for GST?

Businesses with an annual turnover exceeding ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states) are required to register for GST. Additionally, businesses involved in interstate supplies, e-commerce operations, or those liable to pay tax under reverse charge mechanism must register irrespective of turnover threshold.

What are the due dates for filing GST returns?

For regular businesses, GSTR-1 (outward supplies) is due by the 11th of the following month, and GSTR-3B (summary return) is due by the 20th of the following month. For quarterly filers under QRMP scheme, GSTR-1 is due by the 13th of the month following the quarter, and GSTR-3B by the 22nd or 24th depending on the state.

How does Input Tax Credit (ITC) work under GST?

Input Tax Credit allows businesses to claim credit for GST paid on purchases used for business purposes. To claim ITC, the supplier must have filed their returns, the recipient must have received the goods/services, and possess a valid tax invoice. ITC can be utilized to offset output tax liability, reducing the net tax payable.

What happens if I miss a GST filing deadline?

Missing GST filing deadlines results in late fees of ₹50 per day (₹20 per day for nil returns) up to a maximum of ₹10,000. Additionally, interest at 18% per annum is charged on the tax amount due. Repeated non-compliance can lead to penalties, cancellation of registration, and legal action.

When do I need to generate an e-way bill?

An e-way bill is required for movement of goods with value exceeding ₹50,000, whether for supply, return, or other reasons. For interstate movement, e-way bills are mandatory regardless of value. The bill must be generated before the commencement of movement and is valid for varying periods depending on the distance (1-15 days).

How can I reconcile my GST data effectively?

Effective GST reconciliation involves comparing your purchase and sales records with GSTR-2A/2B (auto-populated from supplier returns), identifying mismatches, following up with vendors for corrections, and ensuring all eligible ITC is claimed. Regular reconciliation helps prevent discrepancies, optimize tax credits, and avoid departmental notices.

What documents do I need to provide for GST services?

For GST services, you typically need to provide sales and purchase invoices, e-way bills, credit/debit notes, bank statements, previous GST returns, HSN/SAC codes for your products/services, and details of any reverse charge transactions. For registration, business incorporation documents, address proof, and identity proof of promoters are required.
